Reading the eight

  • A — centered text, hairlines, no image. Quiet but not memorable.
  • B — square thumb + copy. Fits well between New Arrivals and Footer; image gives it visual weight without screaming.
  • C — centered card with short hairlines top/bottom. Looks like a press release.
  • D (quietest) — pull-quote only, no image, no CTA box. Most Hermès. Risk: too quiet, might be missed entirely.
  • E (most editorial) — split-screen, image left, copy right. This is what Hermès / Loewe / The Row actually do for editorial blocks.
  • F (most subtle) — one-line band, reads like a chapter break in a magazine. Works as a between-sections rhythm element.
  • G — postcard card on slightly darker cream. The contrast gives it presence without colour.
  • H (most dramatic) — full-bleed image with bottom-left overlay (mirrors your hero pattern). Strong second visual moment on the page; risk = competes with the hero.

My read: E if you want it to feel like a real journal piece; F if you want the homepage to stay calm and breathable.
