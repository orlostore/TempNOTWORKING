Trade-offs

Existing (left) uses two visual languages on purpose: the editorial cart stepper (subtle, calm) for managing items already in the cart, vs. the coral CTA stepper (loud, brand-coloured) on product cards + PDP where the customer is being asked to act. The split signals "you've decided" vs. "decide now."

Unified (right) applies the modified cart-stepper register everywhere — white fill, navy outline, 12px radius — only the height changes per context (32 / 38 / 48). Pros: one identity, more refined, very Hermès. Cons: loses the coral "you just added something!" affordance on product cards, which was helping signal "this item is now in your cart."

  • If you go unified, the product card needs another signal that the item was added — maybe a small coral dot next to the quantity number, or a brief coral pulse animation when first transformed.
  • The radius (12px) is now consistent across all steppers regardless of which direction you choose — that part's already done.
  • SIGN IN / AS GUEST currently use 8px radius. Raising to 12px aligns them with the steppers; keeping at 8px keeps them feeling like separate decision buttons (slightly different shape language). Mild preference for 12px.
