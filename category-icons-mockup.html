Reading the five

  • A — Current: utilitarian icon-set. Some shapes repeat elsewhere on the site.
  • B — Bespoke ORLO objects (most on-brand): drawings of actual products. Walks the talk of "we are a maker of objects with character." Costs nothing (six SVGs already drawn above). Could expand to 8–10 categories.
  • C — Pure typography (quietest): zero icons, just italic words + numbers. Most Hermès / Aesop. Risk: less scannable on a noisy mobile screen — customers used to icon-based nav may scroll past.
  • D — Italic letterform: vintage monogram feel. Hits a wall when category names share a starting letter (Decor / Desk — flagged). Workable if categories are renamed (Decor → Pieces, Desk → Workspace).
  • E — Postage stamps (most characterful): serrated edges, illustration + italic name + number. Tells the "shipped from Dubai" story without a word. Bolder, but stays editorial because of the hairline + cream + Cormorant treatment.

My read: B for the homepage category band (real products, instantly recognisable, on-brand). E reserved for a special spot — the Shop page filter chips, or a "discover" callout. C if you want the homepage as quiet as possible.
