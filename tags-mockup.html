Reading the sheet

Section 01 picks the visual language (shape + colour). My pick: T1 — navy text on cream rectangle, no rounding, no gradient, 0.55rem caps DM Sans. It's the Hermès register, matches everything else on site (the menu drawer, the accordion labels, the carousel num).

Section 02 picks the sold-out treatment. My pick: S2 — small tag + italic "Sold out" instead of price + the card image stays full-strength (no diagonal line, no grey wash). Cleanest, Hermès-honest. The dim+strikethrough is the Shopify-default look.

Section 03 picks the stacking rule when multiple tags apply. Two viable answers:

  • M1 — one tag wins (cleanest): max one tag visible. Priority: Sold out > New > Bestseller > Handmade. Handmade story lives in product description / "Why imperfect?" accordion, doesn't need a badge.
  • M2 — primary + Handmade secondary: Bestseller/New/Sold out always wins primary slot; Handmade pinned beneath as a smaller hairline outline. Adds character without crowding.

Avoid M6 (three tags stacked) — looks cluttered, fights the editorial register.
