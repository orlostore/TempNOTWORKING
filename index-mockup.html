Comparing the four

MockupPopular NowNew ArrivalsBest for
A — MagazineFull-width auto-carousel · one item at a time, big image, dots indicator. Reads like a magazine cover. Auto-advances every 3.5s, pauses on touch.2-col grid (current pattern)When you have hero-worthy items you want to showcase one by one
B — Boutique2-col grid (current)Horizontal peek-rail · scroll right, next item peeks from the edge. Hermès / Aesop pattern.Encouraging browsing — feels like a curated shelf you swipe through
C — MinimalRemovedRemovedMaximum editorial weight — one "Chapter" of 3 items, then The Edit. Most Hermès, but risks losing product discovery
D — MarqueeInfinite-scroll ribbon · items flow continuously across the screen. Kinetic, draws the eye.2-col grid (smaller, 2 items)Adding rhythm and motion to a quiet page

Other things in every mockup

  • Header — lockup left, "The Edit." centered, search right (live)
  • Hero — Camper Van, bottom-left "Objects with character" (live)
  • The Edit teaser — Variant E split-screen (image left, copy right) — your pick
  • About — accordion, all closed, hairline rows. SEO copy stays in the DOM for Google but doesn't disturb the reader
  • Footer — Customer Enquiries, white card on cream (your pick)

My read: A or B for a working store, C if you want to push hard into the editorial direction (and have the catalogue depth elsewhere — e.g., Shop page), D if you want the homepage to feel alive but at the cost of a quieter Hermès register.
