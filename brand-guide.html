Product Image Guide.

How to shoot, frame, and export product photography so it works consistently across the site, Instagram, and TikTok — without re-shooting for each platform.

Version 01 · 2026

01Background & palette

The site is built on a single cream tone. Match it in the studio and your products will look at home on every surface — product card, hero, carousel, IG grid, Story.

03Composition

  • Subject fills ~75–80% of the frame. The dashed outline in each frame above is the safe area — keep important detail inside it.
  • Centred, with soft natural shadow falling below or behind. Not floating.
  • Keep ~12–18% of margin on all sides for crop flexibility across platforms.
  • One product per frame on the homepage carousel. For Stories / Reels you can layer 2–3 if styled.
  • Same scale across the catalogue — a fridge magnet, a phone, a clock should look proportional to each other so the grid feels coherent.

04Lighting

  • Soft, diffused daylight — north-facing window or a daylight LED through diffusion fabric.
  • One main light, one bounce card (white) opposite. No coloured lights.
  • Soft shadow on cream — never a hard black shadow.
  • Avoid mixed light temperatures (don't mix tungsten + daylight).
  • Same time of day across a shoot — keeps the catalogue tone consistent.

05Do · Don't

Do

  • Cream background, edge to edge
  • Subject in focus, soft shadow
  • Honest colour — what you see is what arrives
  • Show scale — include a hand or known object for Stories
  • Keep imperfections visible if handmade — they're the story

Don't

  • White (#FFF) or grey backgrounds — they'll clash on site
  • Heavy Instagram filters, vintage tints, HDR
  • Drop shadows added in editing — keep it natural
  • Text or stickers in the image itself (overlay them in IG instead)
  • Multiple busy props — one hero subject only

07File naming

Use kebab-case, English, no spaces. Helps both SEO and our backup organisation.

  • vintage-camper-van-red-01.jpg
  • brass-desk-clock-tabletop.jpg
  • polaroid-frame-set-on-shelf.jpg

Number variants -01, -02 if you have multiple angles. Always include the product name first.
