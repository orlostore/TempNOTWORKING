02Two image standards

Every photograph is one of two types. Knowing which before you shoot keeps the catalogue coherent and the homepage editorial.

A — Catalogue (clean)

  • Background: warm beige #E5DCCF. No exceptions. Use studio paper or a calibrated beige seamless.
  • One product per frame, centred, ~75% fill, soft natural shadow.
  • No props, no styling, no narrative. The object alone.
  • Where it shows: product cards, New Arrivals, All Products grid, PDP main image, Instagram feed catalogue posts.

B — Lifestyle (hero)

  • Background: any styled scene. Wood desk, marble, window light, an in-use moment.
  • Tells a story — the gramophone playing, the camper van on an open map, the brass tray with morning coffee.
  • Subject can sit anywhere in the frame (centre, third, bottom-weighted).
  • Where it shows: Popular Now carousel, hero, The Edit, IG Stories & Reels, TikTok, OG share image.

When in doubt: if it lives in a grid → A. If it lives alone with breathing room → B.

Homepage hero — generate TWO sizes, not one

The homepage hero is cropped very differently on desktop vs mobile. If you generate one size and let CSS crop the other, you lose 50–60% of the subject. Always produce two source images per active hero campaign, same scene / same lighting / same palette — different composition.

SurfaceAspectPixel sizeComposition
Desktop hero21 : 9 (cinematic wide)2400 × 1029Subject fills the centre horizontal band — frame is wide, short. Caption sits bottom-left over a darker zone.
Mobile hero4 : 5 (portrait)1080 × 1350Subject centred, more headroom + footroom. Caption sits in the lower third.

Gemini workflow: generate the desktop 2400 × 1029 first (cinematic wide). Then re-prompt: "same scene, same subject, same lighting and palette — recompose as 4:5 portrait 1080 × 1350, subject centred, more headroom and footroom for caption space." Upload both into the Heroes admin — the site picks the right one based on viewport.

Why not 16:9? 16:9 at 1920w forces a 1080px-tall hero — taller than most laptop viewports (1080p, 1440p in browser chrome). You'd have to scroll to see the rest of the page. 21:9 fits comfortably under the fold.

04Gemini prompts (catalogue & lifestyle)

Both image types can be generated with Gemini / Imagen if studio shots aren't available. Catalogue (type A) needs a strict, restrictive prompt — Gemini's default behaviour is to add storytelling and props, which kills the clean grid. Lifestyle (type B) is more permissive but still bounded.

A — Catalogue prompt (strict — copy verbatim)

Replace [PRODUCT] with a short physical description. Do not soften the "MUST NOT" list — that's the entire reason this works. Gemini will sneak props back in the moment you remove an item from the forbidden list.

Studio product photograph of [PRODUCT — e.g., a vintage red metal Volkswagen Beetle toy with hippie flower decals].

Background: a completely flat, solid, untextured single colour fill — #E5DCCF (warm pale taupe, Hermès beige, RGB 229 220 207) — filling 100% of the frame edge to edge. The subject is the ONLY thing in the image.

Lighting: soft, even, top-down studio lighting. Gentle natural contact shadow directly beneath the subject. No other shadows, no light streaks, no window light, no sun beams, no atmosphere, no haze, no dust motes.

Composition: subject centred, sharp, fully in focus, ~15–18% padding on all sides. Photorealistic, fine detail, true colour.

Aspect ratio: 1:1 square, 2000 × 2000 px.

CRITICAL — these MUST NOT appear in the image:
no desk, no table, no surface, no marble, no wood, no fabric, no carpet, no curtains,
no window, no skyline, no city, no architecture, no room, no walls,
no plants, no terracotta pot, no flowers, no leaves,
no sunglasses, no books, no maps, no jewelry, no rings, no coins,
no shadows on the background plane, no gradient on the background, no vignette, no texture,
no props of any kind, no styling, no second object, no people, no hands,
no text, no border, no frame, no signature, no logo, no watermark,
no lifestyle context, no scene, no story.

The subject is alone in a void of solid beige.

Common drift — what Gemini does wrong, and how to push back

  • "Just make it brighter" → Gemini adds windows, skylines, more props. Fix: re-emphasise "solid flat untextured #E5DCCF, no scene, no window."
  • Marble or wood surface sneaks in → cold, modern-apartment feel. Fix: add "no surface, no marble, no wood" to the forbidden list explicitly.
  • Drop shadow under product is gradient, not contact → looks Photoshop-fake. Fix: "soft natural contact shadow only, directly under the subject, no gradient halo."
  • Background colour shifts pink/green/yellow → wrong taupe. Fix: "exact colour #E5DCCF — neither pinkish nor greenish, warm neutral pale taupe."
  • Lifestyle drift (props, curtains, sunglasses, sunflowers) → Gemini interprets "product photo" as "flatlay." Fix: the full MUST NOT list, item by item. Don't shorten it.

B — Lifestyle prompt — master template

Lifestyle images (type B — Popular Now carousel, hero, The Edit, Stories, Reels, TikTok) are generated with Gemini / Imagen. Paste a template, replace [BRACKETED] parts with your product details, and request the 1:1 square first. Then re-prompt the same scene at 9:16 (1080 × 1920) for IG Stories & Reels and TikTok — keeps the same image across platforms.

A high-end editorial lifestyle photograph of a [PRODUCT NAME — short description], in the style of a Hermès or Aesop catalogue page.

Setting: [SCENE — e.g., on a vintage wooden writing desk beside an open paper notebook and a brass paperweight].

Background tones: warm cream, beige, and muted natural neutrals (cream #F8F6F2, warm beige #E5DCCF, soft taupe). No saturated colours. A small navy or muted coral accent on a single detail is allowed if natural to the scene.

Lighting: soft, diffused natural daylight from one side (window light), gentle shadow falling away from the product. Late morning quality. No harsh contrast, no flash.

Composition: subject placed [centred / bottom-left third / on a wooden surface]. Product occupies about 60–70% of frame. Leave breathing room at the top and one side so the same image can be cropped to 9:16 vertical for Stories.

Atmosphere: quiet, considered, editorial — like a printed magazine page. Slight film grain. No people. No hands. No text overlays. No watermarks. No props that compete with the product.

Photorealistic, sharp focus on the product, soft natural shadow, fine detail.

Aspect ratio: 1:1 square, 1080 × 1080.

Always keep in every prompt (consistency anchors)

  • "Style: Hermès or Aesop catalogue page" — anchors the register
  • "Warm cream and beige tones, no saturated colours" — anchors the palette
  • "Soft natural daylight, no harsh contrast" — anchors the lighting
  • "No people, no hands, no text, no watermarks" — clean output
  • "Leave breathing room for 9:16 crop" — same image works on Stories & TikTok
  • "Photorealistic, fine film grain" — keeps it from looking AI-plastic

The two-size workflow

  1. Generate the master image at 1:1 square (1080 × 1080) — that's what goes on the site.
  2. Re-prompt the same scene at 9:16 vertical (1080 × 1920) — for Instagram Stories, Reels, and TikTok. Add to the prompt: "vertical composition, more headroom and footroom, same product and same lighting as previous image."
  3. Save both. Upload square to the site / IG feed, vertical to Stories / Reels / TikTok.

Tip: keep a notes file of prompts that worked — same prompt may need slight rewording in a future Gemini version, but the structure stays.

05Composition

  • Subject fills ~75–80% of the frame. The dashed outline in each frame above is the safe area — keep important detail inside it.
  • Centred, with soft natural shadow falling below or behind. Not floating.
  • Keep ~12–18% of margin on all sides for crop flexibility across platforms.
  • One product per frame on the homepage carousel. For Stories / Reels you can layer 2–3 if styled.
  • Same scale across the catalogue — a fridge magnet, a phone, a clock should look proportional to each other so the grid feels coherent.

06Lighting

  • Soft, diffused daylight — north-facing window or a daylight LED through diffusion fabric.
  • One main light, one bounce card (white) opposite. No coloured lights.
  • Soft shadow on the beige (catalogue) or the surface in shot (lifestyle) — never a hard black shadow.
  • Avoid mixed light temperatures (don't mix tungsten + daylight).
  • Same time of day across a shoot — keeps the catalogue tone consistent.

07Do · Don't

Do

  • Catalogue: warm beige #E5DCCF edge to edge
  • Subject in focus, soft shadow
  • Honest colour — what you see is what arrives
  • Show scale — include a hand or known object for Stories
  • Keep imperfections visible if handmade — they're the story

Don't

  • Pure white (#FFF), grey, or page cream (#F8F6F2) as the product bg — all wrong
  • Heavy Instagram filters, vintage tints, HDR
  • Drop shadows added in editing — keep it natural
  • Text or stickers in the image itself (overlay them in IG instead)
  • Multiple busy props — one hero subject only
  • For AI-generated catalogue shots specifically: no marble or stone surfaces (reads cold/modern, wrong register), no city skylines through windows (says "Dubai apartment" not "ORLO object"), no sunflowers / dried flowers / terracotta pots as props (Gemini default flatlay clichés), no curtain / window-light effects (catalogue should be flat top-down studio)

09Dimension / spec card

Some products (toys, decor, miniatures) benefit from a small spec card showing length × width × height. This is a variant of the clean catalogue image (type A) — same exact background, same lighting, with discreet dimension annotations added on top.

Visual standard

  • Background: warm beige #E5DCCF, same as the catalogue card. Never white — white reads marketplace, breaks brand register.
  • Annotation lines: thin navy hairlines (#1A3A52, 1 px), with small arrow caps at each end. Lines run alongside the product, not over it.
  • Numbers: DM Sans, weight 400, navy, all-caps, ~14 px on a 2000 px square. Format: 12 CM or 12 × 6 × 5 CM.
  • Caption strip (optional, bottom): Cormorant italic 300, navy, ~22 px on 2000 sq, format "Dimensions · L 12 cm × W 6 cm × H 5 cm".
  • Where it shows: PDP image #2 or #3 in the scroll, IG carousel slide 2 (slide 1 = catalogue, slide 2 = spec), TikTok product details overlay.

Generation workflow — two options

Recommended: Gemini generates the clean catalogue image, then you overlay the numbers/lines in Canva, Figma, or any image editor — using the brand fonts (DM Sans for numbers, Cormorant italic for the caption). Why: Gemini renders text unreliably for precise numbers, so trusting it to print "12 cm" can give you "1Z cm" or "I2 c.m." Overlaying manually = guaranteed accuracy.

Faster but less precise: ask Gemini directly. Use the prompt below, fill in [LENGTH/WIDTH/HEIGHT] with your actual numbers. Check the output carefully — re-prompt if any digit is wrong.

Gemini prompt — spec card (attach the clean catalogue image)

Before pasting: replace the three numbers below (LENGTH_CM, WIDTH_CM, HEIGHT_CM) with your actual values — just the numerals, e.g. 12. Do not wrap them in brackets, quotes, or anything else — Gemini will literally render whatever characters you give it.

I'm attaching a clean catalogue product photograph on a warm beige #E5DCCF background. Use this exact image as the base — do NOT redraw, restyle, or alter the product or background in any way.

Add a discreet technical dimension overlay to this image, in the style of a Hermès or Aesop product spec page:

— A thin navy hairline (colour #1A3A52, 1 px wide) running horizontally below the product, with small arrow caps at both ends, indicating the LENGTH. Label the line with the text "LENGTH_CM CM" in DM Sans, weight 400, navy #1A3A52, all uppercase, ~14 px size, positioned just below the line, centred. The label must be the number followed by the word CM — for example "24 CM" — with no brackets, no quotes, no extra punctuation.

— A thin navy hairline running vertically to the right of the product, with small arrow caps at both ends, indicating the HEIGHT. Label with "HEIGHT_CM CM" in the same font and colour, again just the number followed by CM, no brackets or punctuation around it.

— (Optional, if product has depth) a third thin navy hairline at a slight diagonal indicating WIDTH/DEPTH, labelled "WIDTH_CM CM" — same rule, number followed by CM only.

— Bottom strip caption, centred under the product: "Dimensions · L LENGTH_CM cm × W WIDTH_CM cm × H HEIGHT_CM cm" in Cormorant Garamond italic, weight 300, navy #1A3A52, ~22 px size. Substitute the numerals directly — no brackets, no quotes around the numbers in the final caption.

Keep everything else identical to the attached image. The beige background, the product, the lighting, the shadow — unchanged. Only add the annotations.

CRITICAL — do NOT add: no white background swap, no grey, no rulers, no grid, no measurement icons, no second product view, no exploded diagram, no props, no text other than the three dimension labels and the caption strip, no watermarks, no logos, no square brackets [ ], no curly braces { }, no angle brackets < >, no quotation marks around the numbers.

Aspect ratio: 1:1 square, 2000 × 2000 px.

Worked example: for a Beetle that is 24 cm long × 9 cm wide × 9 cm tall, every LENGTH_CM becomes 24, every WIDTH_CM becomes 9, every HEIGHT_CM becomes 9. The labels in the final image then read "24 CM", "9 CM", "9 CM" and the caption reads "Dimensions · L 24 cm × W 9 cm × H 9 cm".

Quick reference values (when you fill in the brackets)

  • Use centimetres only (cm), never inches — UAE-first.
  • Round to whole numbers unless the half matters (so 12 cm, not 12.3 cm).
  • Order is always Length × Width × Height (L × W × H), front-to-back × side-to-side × top-to-bottom.
  • If the product is essentially flat (a magnet, a card), use L × W only and drop the height line.

10Video — Reels, TikTok & Stories

Vertical short-form video on Instagram Reels, TikTok, and IG Stories is the most powerful surface ORLO has. The format is locked so every reel feels like the same brand — the colour, the type, the end card. Concepts vary; the frame around them doesn't.

Palette (matches the site exactly — no separate "video palette")

  • Cream background #F8F6F2 — the same cream the page uses. Never a different shade for video.
  • Navy #1A3A52 — primary text, divider lines, pills.
  • Coral / Orange #E76F51 — accent, CTAs, URL, Arabic tagline, trust circles.
  • Grey #A0A5AA — secondary captions / Arabic descriptions in trust badges.
  • Dark warm #14100C — only for mystery / silhouette tease scenes, never as default bg.

Fonts (locked, no substitutes)

  • English — DM Sans · weights 500, 700, 800. Used for: system captions, taglines, CTAs, URL, trust badges, all-caps tracked labels. The "voice of the brand" font.
  • English — Cormorant Garamond italic · weights 300, 400 (italic only). Used for: product names, scene headlines, editorial pull-quotes, the "Edit" wordmark. This is the same italic serif the site uses on product cards and hero titles — it's what makes the brand read as magazine, not e-commerce. Use it whenever a product is being named or a scene is making a poetic statement (not a system message).
  • Arabic — Almarai · weights 400, 700, 800. The ONLY Arabic font for ORLO. Never Noto Kufi, never Tajawal, never system Arabic.
  • When to choose which (English):
    • "QUALITY IS A CLICK AWAY" → DM Sans 500, all-caps, tracked (system tagline)
    • "Vintage Camper Van" → Cormorant Garamond italic 300 (product name, editorial)
    • "COMING SOON" → DM Sans 800 (system label in the navy pill)
    • "Meet the Buddies" → Cormorant Garamond italic 400 (editorial scene headline)
    • "Free Delivery" / trust badge labels → DM Sans 500 (functional system text)
    • "orlostore.com" → DM Sans 700 (functional URL)
  • Mixing in one scene: never use Cormorant italic + DM Sans on the same line. Stack them — Cormorant italic for the editorial headline, DM Sans below it (smaller) for the support line or AR translation cue.
  • Critical render note: when generating frames in Python/PIL, use raw Arabic strings with Almarai directly. Do NOT use arabic_reshaper + python-bidi — Almarai handles RTL and letter-joining natively via OpenType. Double-processing reverses the letters.
  • Separator: if a punctuation separator is needed in Arabic, use the Arabic comma ، not the Latin mid-dot · (mid-dot renders as a tofu box in Arabic).

Visual rules (LOCKED — every video)

  • Hard cuts only. No crossfades between scenes. Crossfades read as travel-vlog, not editorial.
  • Logo rock-solid throughout at 100% opacity once parked in the corner.
  • UAE flag colours intact inside the logo — never desaturate, never re-tint.
  • Almarai is the only Arabic font. Repeated for emphasis.
  • Silhouettes for tease/reveal are pure black (#000) on cream — no grey, no transparency tricks.
  • Bilingual everywhere: every headline that appears in EN gets an AR line directly below it. Same beat, same screen time, same weight.
  • Pacing: 15–16 seconds total. Don't overstuff. If a concept needs more, cut a scene.
